CEH — who it works for in India

CEH is the right choice if: you're entering ethical hacking (0–3 years experience), your target companies are Indian IT services (TCS, Wipro, Infosys, HCL), you're targeting government or PSU cybersecurity roles (many RFPs require CEH explicitly), or you want to study theory-first and need structured content. The MCQ exam format means you can pass CEH with 2–3 months of focused study without a home lab. EC-Council's authorized training centers are available across Indian cities — Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune.

OSCP — who it works for in India

OSCP is the right choice if: you already have 2–3 years security experience, you want to move into red team or senior penetration testing (₹18–40L+ roles), your target is MNCs, FinTech, or global security consultancies (Deloitte Cyber, KPMG Cyber, PwC Cyber, CrowdStrike India), or you're willing to do the intensive lab work required. The 24-hour practical exam is respected globally precisely because it cannot be faked — you either own the machines or you don't.

Career path: CEH → OSCP → senior security roles

Years 1–3: CompTIA Security+ → CEH → SOC Analyst or junior pen tester role (₹4–8L)
Years 3–5: OSCP → Senior Penetration Tester or Red Team Analyst (₹10–22L)
Years 5–8: OSCP + CISSP → Security Architect or CISO track (₹22–40L)
Years 8+: CISO + IIM Indore AI & Cybersecurity → CISO or Head of Security (₹40–70L+)
